I often say I don't play games anymore, but I'm making an effort to change that. I keep buying gaming hardware that goes unused. I'm also terrible about taking time for me, for my own relaxation and enjoyment.

So alright! I keep putting "play more games" on my new years resolutions. Time to do that.

You can view all entries in this series via the [game-club](/tags/game-club/) tag.


## MiSTer FPGA
I became a fan of the MiSTer FPGA project last year. By years end I owned 2 DE10-Nano's. The first I kitted out with the usually addon boards you find on eBay, but for the 2nd I managed to get one of the first batch MiSTer Multisystem kits from Retro Man Cave. It arrived before years end, and after Christmas I slapped a 256 GB MicroSD card in each and started filling them with games.

The first was a [collection of 300 DOS games](https://archive.org/details/top-300-final) I found on `archive.org`. Most of the games come pre-configured to run, though a handful don't start. It's unclear if the MiSTer "486" core is to blame, or what exactly.

Next I grabbed the latest version of GameBase64 (v17). It's a comprehensive collection of Commodore 64 games.

Tosec 1G1R.

It's a tech demo. It's gorgeous, a bit cheesy, but it's still a tech demo.

The PlayStation 5 likes to turn on and take focus (HDMI CEC) whenever it detects that switch-box is on the TV. Last "game" I played was The Matrix tech demo, so that's what pops up.


## Civilization (MS DOS)
Probably the first game I played on my new MiSTer + Multisystem. New years eve Jenna and I stayed up playing Civ, her on the latest (Civilization 6) and myself on the original. I revisited the game a few times in the days that followed, but after a crushing victory I was satisfied.


## Neuromancer (Commodore 64)
One of my favorite games, so much that I've been planning a playthrough video documentary on it. Neuromancer is a greatly underappreciated cyberpunk adventure game. I made a "Let's Play" video some years ago, and I've been meaning to redo it since re-reading William Gibson's books.

I didn't play it very long, an hour at most, but it was enough to get me thinking about the doc again.

## Monster World 4 (Sega Genesis)
I googled online for Metroidvania recommendations for the Sega Genesis. I know Sega had RPG's, but not necessarily Metroidvania's. I'd played the remake of "Wonder Boy in Monster World" some years back, but I'd never played this.

As Metroidvania's go it was good. The game defied my expectations a few times. Not that it blew my mind or anything, but it started out quite _simple_. _You're a hero right, okay, we trust you. Aren't these things cute?_

Multiple times I was ready to take to Twitter and chew-out the game for being unfair, but then I'd miraculously finish what irked me with minimal effort. Or later in the game I discovered where to farm gold, but the seller of the best items left the town... until he was suddenly back again with better items.

By game end, I'd say the only unfair part was the Sky Temple. It took some _mad_ platforming to finish it, almost as bad as late game Hollow Knight.

And I would almost say that since you couldn't buy the "full-life" potions, that made the ending unfair, except it didn't. The final boss was a pushover. The final world is basically a boss rush, except I found out later the bosses stay dead.

It's a cute game overall. I enjoyed it.


## Other games
### Commodore 64
* Equinox - An clumsy exploration thrust-platformer. It's one of those "short on instructions" games. I eventually figured out there was a credit system, and to use the transporter you need to spend a credit (though you could in some cases exit without consuming the credit). I wont say the game is buggy, but I will say it's quirky. The game is also a bit unfair, as you need to complete 8 levels with only 2 extra lives. I'd say I got as far as the 4th level.
* Mission Impossible - An old favorite. Played it a few times, solved maybe 1 puzzle, and grew tired of it.
* Mission Impossible 2 - I'd never played this before. There's definitely more to this game, but also feels a bit more structured. I played maybe one round, but that lasted a good 30+ minutes.
### MS DOS
* Goblins 3 - Annoyingly the first two Goblins games weren't working (Gobliiins). I'll check the Amiga another day... at least I'm assuming there's Amiga heritage here. It's a neat game, funny in places, but clearly a product of its time. There's a point in the game where the world _sort of_ opens up, and I got that far.
