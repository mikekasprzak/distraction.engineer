I enjoy creating identities.

In practice, having a name for something is so useful. Aside from the obvious "knowing what to call it", a name can provide direction. When in doubt, what would "name" do? For example, a video game called `"Smiles"` should have a cheerful "smiling" art style (something Death Smiles doesn't understand).

`The Distration.Engineer` is a recent creation. A "creative identity", something chosen in 2021 while struggling to condense my interests and experience into as few words words as possible. My previous creative identity was `Sykhronics`, which eventually became `Sykhronics Entertainment` my defunct indie game studio. After 20+ years  . Some of my considerations include:

* I've spent most my life tied to video games, defining myself as a game creator. I think it's funny calling video games "distractions".
* I built and run a social network (AKA a community), which are extremely popular distractions today.
* I regularly "distract" creators with the events I run (Ludum Dare).
* I "entertain", but the term "entertainer" typically referrs to performers that entertain, not people that create entertainment.
* I engineer things. Not only have I built dozens of video games, but I've built the low-level systems and architected the infrastructure that powers them. Graphics, netcode, physics, toolchains, databases, clients, servers, apps, porting, I've done so much that it's geninely difficult to describe.
* Out of necessity I made the jump from a highly opinionated C/C++/Assembly programmer to web programmer, and liked it.
* The `.engineer` domain extension was calling out to me.
* `Distraction Engineer` is a name an English speaking person can read and spell! That might sound odd but imagine growing up with the last name "Kasprzak", choosing a creative identity "Sykhronics", and being known for something called "Ludum Dare". You get tired of having to correct people.
* An ADHD person might be considered "distracted", and I think the sub-identity `The Distracted.Engineer` makes a good pun.
