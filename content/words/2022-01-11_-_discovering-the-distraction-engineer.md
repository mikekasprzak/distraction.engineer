I think it's healthy to reinvent yourself.

We do it a little every year with New Year's resolutions. Maybe you take a step back and you don't like what you see. Maybe you don't know what you see. Whatever it is, it could be better.

I crossed into my 40's during _the lost year_ (2020). Leading up to it, I left a job making video games for a friend. Classic millennial problem, I wasn't making enough "progress financially" for retirement and all the adult things you're supposed to plan for. I was still obsessed with games, but 20 years deep, my trust in games as a career was waning. 

In the short term I had some filmmaking I wanted to do. I took a stab a [short film](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OtWv8xO94dE), a [mini documentary](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iu4805p1yPo), and helped a friend with his [Kickstarter](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/dereklaufman/creations-vol1). I picked up the video hobby the year before when I saw a need. I enjoy it. It's something I want to do more.

After that, my plan was to find a well paying job that either didn't eat all my time or my brain, while _on the side_ learn Houdini and Unreal, so I can begin crafting my indie action mind-fuckery dream game.

Resume updated, applications sent and...hey, what's a Corona-virus?

Yes, I was job hunting at the beginning of a pandemic. Nobody knew what's going on, and now nobody was hiring.

> _What a horrible night to have a curse._

It was obvious finding work would take a while.

Plan B.

I had a bit of money left, plus no shortage of ambition, so I decided if I couldn't find a job, I'd make a job.

I was starting a company.


## Interactively Snacking

A company name I've been sitting on was **Interactive Snacks**. After the mouthful that was **Sykhronics**, I wanted something more playful and _actually spellable_.

My plan initially was to build a company focused on [Instant Games](https://www.fortressofdoors.com/the-future-of-games-is-an-instant-flash-to-the-past/). I forget what I was calling them, but I saw an opportunity. 

This time I would incorporate. Sykhronics began as a personal branding, but I used the name for my business, a sole proprietorship, and I ran that for a decade. I could have done more _regular_ business things with it, I made an effort to keep the business small, simple, and solo. I hate doing taxes. The last thing I wanted to do was **TWO** taxes. A sole proprietorship came with other limitations that became more obvious once Canada's small business covid relief benefits entered the picture. It's bananas, but a corporation existing for one year gets access to credit, loans, government support programs, not to mention a lower taxation rate (though if exercised as dividends, at least in Canada it works out about the same).

On July 1st, 2020, [**Interactive Snacks Limited**](https://interactivesnacks.com) was incorporated.

I spent many months before and after incorporation applying to startup business programs, looking for funding like a _proper_ company. I made some great headway, learned a lot, did as well as I could, but "video games" as a business activity would not sell.

After months of planning and pitching, I think I was finally ready to give up on games.


## I ludum DARE you

Even before my ["I'm a C++ programmer"](/words/how-blogging-helped-me-break-up-with-c) identity crisis, I was having my "I'm a Game Developer" identity crisis. I struggled with it for years.

When I was young I aspired to make games. As a teenager I did, and later I dropped out of college to take a job making games. 

I could't complain. I got exactly what I wanted. I was making games for a living.

The problem was I wasn't making the games _I_ wanted to, and finding the time and the motivation while holding a day job was hard. So some friends and I started game jamming, scheduling weekends we'd get together to cheer each-other on and do it, and that became Ludum Dare. Years passed and Geoff's forum (the OG "Ludum Dare") was long gone, but the event it inspired carried on.

Responsibility for Ludum Dare eventually fell to me, which was probably good for it, since it turns out I was _crazy enough_ to keep it going for (coming up) 20 years. My strange adopted digital child.

For years, Ludum Dare was just a thing I did. Like any recreational activity, some people play-in or run sports leagues, I ran a "sports league" for game makers.

Then one day, it got popular.

When I first attended the Game Developer's Conference in 2009, I was as _Mike the Game Developer, with a puzzle game in the Independent Games Festival (Mobile)_. When I last attended GDC in 2019, I was _Mike the Ludum Dare guy_.

Again my aspirations as a kid were to be a _"Game Developer"_, not a _"Game Developer Developer"_. It fascinated me the position I found myself in, and while I was honoured and proud, I couldn't let go of "Mike the Game Developer". That's who I wanted to be, and I couldn't shake it.

By the end of 2020, I didn't trust game development, not anymore. Sure, I could have continued to find work, but in my eyes this was gambling, and the deck was stacked against me. It was going to take miraculous luck and motivation to thrive, and I was all out of both.


## I ludum DARE you to try again

After a nudge from a friend, I decided _fuck-it_, changed my focus to Ludum Dare, and pivoted Interactive Snacks to be a company on a mission to support Ludum Dare and its community.

I don't mean to belittle my effort in games, but making a business focused on Ludum Dare had _obvious_ potential. I jokingly call it easy mode, if you consider working weeks straight of late nights and weekends easy. No, it's the right amount of difficult, it's the gaming business that's unreasonable.

I'm in a weird place. As a business person I'm no longer failing, and it's thanks to the thing that got in the way of my first business.

I no longer pay the bills by making games. I'm not sure I ever will again.

That's not to say I wont continue making games, but it feels like that's no longer my career.

Who am I then? What am I?

## The Distraction.Engineer

I like naming things, so lets give it a go.

If I'm not a Game Developer, what am I? What do I do?

I've made games, I've inspired with events, I've entertained.

I'm obsessed with systems and creating. I like building.

As a creator I've never liked being told to _stay in my lane_.

What's a fun way to describe this?

As you noticed, I decided to name this next generation of Mike, the post "Game Developer Mike" generation as "Mike the Distraction.Engineer", or simply **_The Distraction.Engineer_**.

I think it's a catchy name, but I also thought Sykhronics was cool (I still do).


## What is the Distraction.Engineer?

A modus operandi, and this blog.

In time I'd like to use it as my personal branding, like how I used Sykhronics before it became my defunct game company.

Whenever we return to a world where we meet IRL again, I'd like to design and carry cards or stickers for it.

Heck, I might publish some music here.

The Distraction.Engineer is whatever I want it to be. Stay a while and listen.
