I've always loved writing. It's probably the best thing I do for my ADHD scatterbrain. Committing words to page forces me confront thoughts and ideas, to flesh them out or discard them. When I finish writing I feel accomplished... except when I'm writing for social media. I don't like how social media makes me feel.

The early days of "Twitter" had a vibe akin to an unstructured forum. One feed with many active topics. It was a great way to keep up with friends and share. Today X-Twitter no better than a chatroom, plagued by volume and algorithms that every post is fleeting, gone in an instant. Switching to a small social network like Mastodon seemed the solution. With less volume and less fat it'd be easier to follow. What I didn't anticipate the the bad habits we learned along the way.

To its credit and detriment, Mastodon felt like a place you could be raw. With 500 characters, brevity and careful editing were no longer necessary. A stream of consciousness could exit your fingertips, be published, and get shared immediately. This might not sound like a problem, but the larger raw'er Mastodon posts are harder to consume. You can't just glance, you need to read and expand posts to know if they were even worth reading. Not to mention, the "Content Warning" feature is constantly abused for engagement. Who can resist wanting to know a secret?

In short, Mastodon is "worse blogging".

That's not to say Mastodon and X-Twitter don't have value. When broadcasting to an audience, they're still far more effective than Discord and Reddit, but today I don't think participation is necessary. The era where you _had_ to be on "Twitter" to connect and network is over. Whether you need on X-Twitter today is debatable, but no social media silo is what it used to be, and wont be ever again. 

The [canary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sentinel_species) is [dead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twitter#Rebrand_to_X). For your health, maybe you shouldn't be a miner?
